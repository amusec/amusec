Vivek Kumar / Still Curious

This is not a resume.

I am a Computer Science graduate from Lovely Professional University, shaped by a Diploma in CSE from 2019 to 2022, and years of building things before I fully understood them.

Diploma in CSE 2019-2022 LPU CSE Graduate 2022-2025

Class 7. A textbook. One page.

I found a chapter about HTML in a school textbook.

It was the first time I heard the word webpage. I did not know what it meant, but I could not stop thinking about it.

000webhost / .tk / first website

I did not understand domains.
So I bought one.

Internet prices were high. I found 000webhost, hosted a site, then discovered free .tk domains. The words were confusing. The feeling was clear.

PHP / MySQL / servers

Website builders were everywhere.
I wanted to build one myself.

PHP MySQL Dynamic content YouTube tutorials

I failed because I did not understand what I was connecting, or why. Then slowly, something worked.

2019 / Arduino / tinkering

Then Arduino entered my life.

Sensors. Breadboards. Small projects for personal problems. The screen was no longer enough; I wanted to touch the systems I was building.

Diploma in CSE

I chose the branch that matched my curiosity.

In the first year, I found hacking, systems, and the thrill of understanding what was hidden underneath.

Then Covid happened.

The world stopped.

My learning accelerated.

First real security lesson

I learned what access really means.

During Covid, I discovered serious weaknesses in a board website and saw how a small mistake could expose a much larger system. I tried to find the right person to report it to, but never found a clear channel. That moment changed how I looked at security.

Static IP / BSNL / port forwarding / SMTP / hosting

My bedroom became a datacenter.

I took a BSNL connection with a static IP, coordinated to open ports 80, 443, and SMTP, and started hosting from home. Networking stopped being theory.

I wanted to build a hosting company from my bedroom.

The CPU overheated.

So I started again with Raspberry Pi, hosting, protocols, and live traffic. Failure did not stop the experiment. It made it sharper.

I stopped asking

“How do I use this?”

and started asking

“How does this work?”

Security Networking APIs Reverse Engineering

Tools for strange problems

If a problem annoyed me, I built something for it.

Command-line helpers, traffic experiments, API automation, replicated network requests, and small local agents. The deeper I went, the more I learned that curiosity needs judgment.

German project

A senior and I delivered a Webflow exporter.

  • Links broke.
  • Assets disappeared.
  • Forms stopped working.
  • A small task became a funded project.
When a problem is alive,
I can’t sleep.

€5000+

Split between three people.

And most of it went back into more experiments.

College reopened / IoT instructor / Teaching C

Four days.
₹32,000.

An IoT instructor asked for a company website. We delivered. Then he hired me to train graduates in C. I learned fast, taught faster, and walked out believing again.

Diploma finished / LPU / CSE

Then graduation started.

A new campus. A bigger room. More learning. More comparison. The same curiosity following me everywhere.

Fourth year / the confidence gap

I felt like I knew nothing.

I did not even dare to sit for interviews. No job came. The skill was there, but the confidence was missing.

After graduation / USA client

A leaked credential opened a new door.

While exploring, I found exposed credentials on a website and reported the issue. That led to a person from the USA, testing work, bug hunting, and my first steady $600/month remote opportunity.

NandGate

Then silence.
Then another project.

After three quiet months, work started again. This time it became a proper role through NandGate, building and testing a real project until it ended three months later.

My laptop

For most people, it is a laptop.

My first website My first server My first project My first client My first euro My first security finding My first remote client My first monthly dollar income Thousands of experiments Thousands of failures

For me, it is where curiosity lives.

I was not in love with websites.

I was not in love with servers.

I was not in love with cloud.

I was in love with understanding.

What I am searching for now

Somewhere skills matter.

Challenges are real.

Contributions matter.

And curiosity is not treated like a distraction.

I’m not looking for a job.

I’m looking for the next problem that keeps me awake at 3 AM.

Still Curious. Still Building. Still Learning.
