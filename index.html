Read My Story
MY JOURNEY

This isn't a portfolio.

It's a collection of experiments, failures, late nights and curiosity.

2017

The HTML Chapter

Class 7.

A chapter about HTML appeared in my school textbook.

Most students moved on after finishing the lesson.

I couldn't stop thinking about it.

The idea that anyone could create a webpage and make it accessible to the world felt unreal.

Internet was expensive. Resources were limited.

Curiosity wasn't.

I found 000webhost.

Then I found free .tk domains.

I still didn't understand domains, hosting, DNS or servers.

I just wanted to know more.

2018

Learning Through Confusion

Website builders were becoming popular.

Click a few buttons. Get a website.

I wanted to understand what happened behind those buttons.

So I started learning PHP.

Most tutorials made absolutely no sense.

I copied code. Broke things. Fixed things. Broke them again.

Eventually something worked.

I wasn't learning because I understood. I understood because I kept building.
2019

Hardware Changes Everything

Websites weren't enough anymore.

I wanted to touch the systems I was building.

Arduino opened an entirely new world.

Sensors. Breadboards. Experiments. Endless curiosity.

Every project answered one question and created ten more.

Eventually I joined Diploma in Computer Science Engineering.

2020

The Covid Years

Then Covid happened.

The world slowed down.

My learning accelerated.

Google Cloud. Virtual Machines. Linux. Networking. Hosting. Security.

I convinced my family to get a BSNL connection with a static IP.

My dream was simple:

Build a hosting company from my bedroom.

I opened ports. Hosted websites. Ran mail servers. Broke things. Fixed things.

Networking stopped being theory.

I could see packets moving. Requests arriving. Systems communicating.

My bedroom slowly turned into a datacenter.

Then the CPU overheated and died.

Weeks of work disappeared.

Failure taught me more than success.

2020-2021

Curiosity Goes Deeper

Somewhere during Covid, the questions changed.

I stopped asking:

How do I use this?

And started asking:

How does this work underneath?

APIs. Network traffic. Authentication. Security. Reverse engineering. Automation.

Every answer revealed another layer.

2021

The Problem That Refused To Die

A senior and I got an opportunity to build a Webflow website exporter.

It sounded simple.

Export HTML. Export CSS. Export images.

Done.

Except it wasn't.

Links broke. Assets disappeared. Forms stopped working.

The challenge wasn't coding.

The challenge was understanding how Webflow itself worked.

Three nights. Almost no sleep.

One day I couldn't even stand properly.

After recovering, I came back immediately.

When a problem is alive, I can't sleep.

Eventually it worked.

The project became funded by a German agency.

More than €5000 was earned and split among three people.

2022

Teaching & Graduation

An IoT instructor needed a website.

We built it.

Then he asked me to teach graduates C programming.

Four days. ₹32,000.

The same year, I started my Bachelor's in Computer Science Engineering.

2024-2025

The Confidence Gap

By the final year, I felt like I knew nothing.

Years of learning. Years of building.

Yet somehow everyone else looked more qualified.

Looking back, it wasn't a skill problem.

It was a confidence problem.

2025

NandGate

After graduation, curiosity helped again.

A security finding led to conversations.

Those conversations led to projects.

Those projects led to NandGate.

Once again, I was getting paid to solve problems.

What Drives Me

Curiosity
Reverse Engineering
Systems Thinking
Hard Problems

I'm not looking for a job.

I'm looking for the feeling.

The feeling of staring at a problem at 3 AM.

The feeling of having absolutely no idea how something works.

The feeling of finally making it work after days of failure.

I don't miss the money. I miss those nights.

And I'm looking for them again.
